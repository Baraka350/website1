Typing speed

Typing speed refers to the rate at which a person can type text, usually measured in words per minute (WPM). It indicates how quickly someone can input data or type written content using a keyboard. Typing speed is an important skill, particularly in tasks that require significant text entry, such as data entry, transcription, or writing documents. Higher typing speeds often lead to increased productivity and efficiency.

Typing Speed Improvement Tips

1. Learn Touch Typing

Touch typing means typing without looking at the keyboard. It helps build muscle memory, enabling faster and more accurate typing.

Practice by focusing on proper finger placement and avoid looking at the keyboard while typing.

2. Use Proper Finger Placement

Keep your fingers on the home row (ASDF for the left hand and JKL; for the right hand) at all times. This position is the starting point for typing most letters.

Your thumbs should rest on the spacebar.

3. Maintain Correct Posture

  • Sit upright with your back straight and your elbows at a 90-degree angle.
  • Keep your wrists straight and avoid resting them on the desk while typing.
  • Your eyes should be level with the top of your screen to reduce neck strain.

4. Practice Regularly

Like any other skill, practice is key. The more you practice, the more your typing speed and accuracy will improve.

Use online typing tools like 10fastfingers, Key Hero, or TypeRacer to practice and track your progress.

5. Focus on Accuracy First

Accuracy is more important than speed, especially in the beginning. Focus on typing correctly and make fewer mistakes, which will naturally increase your speed over time.

Aim for 95% or higher accuracy. Speed will increase as accuracy becomes consistent.

6. Familiarize Yourself with the Keyboard Layout

The more familiar you are with the layout of the keyboard, the faster you will type. This includes knowing the locations of keys and their functions without looking at them.

7. Use All Fingers

Instead of using just a few fingers, try to use all fingers, including your pinky fingers, for typing. This will significantly increase your typing speed and reduce strain on your hands.

Practice typing exercises that involve all fingers for each key.

8. Avoid Looking at the Keyboard

Make a conscious effort not to look at the keyboard while typing. This forces your fingers to rely on muscle memory.

If you need to look, take a glance quickly, but try not to rely on it.

9. Take Breaks

Avoid typing for extended periods without breaks. Take regular breaks (e.g., every 30 minutes) to relax your hands and wrists and prevent fatigue.

10. Use Typing Games or Challenges

Engaging in typing games or challenges can make practicing more fun and motivating.

Games like TypeRacer or TypingClub let you race against others or set personal goals.

11. Increase Your Vocabulary

The more words you recognize instantly, the faster you'll be able to type them. Reading frequently and practicing common word patterns can help improve your typing fluency.

12. Gradually Increase Speed

Start typing slowly to build accuracy and comfort. As you become more confident, start increasing your speed gradually while maintaining accuracy.

Typing Speed Metrics Table

Typing Speed Type Description Formula
Words Per Minute (WPM) Measures how many words a typist can type in one minute. A "word" is generally considered to be 5 characters (including spaces). WPM = (Total Characters Typed / 5) / Time in Minutes
Net Words Per Minute (NWPM) Takes into account the errors made while typing. This is the most accurate representation of typing speed. NWPM = ((Total Characters Typed - Errors) / 5) / Time in Minutes
Net Effective Words Per Minute (NEPM) Considers not only the errors but also the time spent correcting them. It adjusts for correction time. NEPM = ((Total Correct Characters - Error Correction Time) / 5) / Time in Minutes
Keystrokes Per Minute (KPM) Measures the total number of keystrokes made, whether correct or incorrect. Includes all typed keys, not just the words. KPM = Total Keystrokes / Time in Minutes
Net Keystrokes Per Minute (NKPM) Like KPM, but takes into account typing errors. Counts only the effective keystrokes and deducts mistakes. NKPM = (Total Keystrokes - Errors) / Time in Minutes
Characters Per Minute (CPM) Measures the total number of characters typed per minute, including spaces and punctuation. CPM = Total Characters Typed / Time in Minutes
